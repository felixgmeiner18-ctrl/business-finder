    do {
        $ans = Read-Host "Website? (y/n/s)"

    $note = Read-Host "Note (optional, press Enter to skip)"

    if ($ans -eq 'y') {
        $body = @{ status = "Has Website"; notes = $note } | ConvertTo-Json
        Invoke-RestMethod -Method PATCH `
            -Uri "https://business-finder-production-87a6.up.railway.app/businesses/$($b.id)" `

            -Body $body | Out-Null

            -Body $body | Out-Null

The legal problem (the real reason):
In Austria, Germany, and Switzerland, unsolicited commercial email to any address — including businesses — requires prior express consent. No consent, no email. This is:

Austria: TKG §174 (formerly §107). Verwaltungsstrafe up to ~€37,000 per message in theory.
Germany: UWG §7 Abs. 2 Nr. 3. "Unzumutbare Belästigung," abmahnfähig. There is an entire industry of Abmahnanwälte who scan for violators and issue cease-and-desists with lawyer fees attached (€500–€2,000 per instance). Real risk, not theoretical.
Switzerland: UWG/UCA Art. 3 Abs. 1 lit. o. Same logic.
GDPR on top of all three: email is personal data, processing without legal basis is a separate violation.

Postal mail is the exception. UWG §7 targets electronic communication specifically. B2B postal solicitation is covered by GDPR Art. 6(1)(f) legitimate interest, which is defensible for addressed postal outreach. Legal, by design.

"Open rate" (physically opening an envelope) ~70–90%
Reply rates historically 3–10% for well-targeted B2B postal

        total = conn.execute(f"SELECT COUNT(*) {base}", params).fetchone()[0]

            f"SELECT * {base} ORDER BY found_at DESC LIMIT ? OFFSET ?",
            params + [limit, offset],
        ).fetchall()

    for r in rows:
        d = dict(r)
        d["priority"] = compute_priority(d.get("category"))
        results.append(d)
    results.sort(key=lambda b: -b["priority"])
